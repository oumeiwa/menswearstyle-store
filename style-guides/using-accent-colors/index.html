Using Accent Colors Without Looking Loud

How to add burgundy, green, blue and other accents through small pieces instead of loud outfits.

In This Guide

  1. Overview
  2. What To Check
  3. Common Mistakes
  4. Buying Checklist

Overview

Accent color works best when it has a job. A burgundy tie, green sweater or dusty blue shirt can add interest without taking over.

Muted colors are easier than bright ones. Forest green, wine, rust and slate blue pair naturally with navy, gray and brown.

What To Check

Use one accent at a time if you are unsure. Multiple competing colors can make a simple outfit feel busy.

Repeat color subtly through related tones rather than exact matches. This looks more natural and less staged.

Common Mistakes

The easiest mistake is buying the idea of an item instead of judging how it works on your body, in your climate and with your existing wardrobe.

Another mistake is ignoring maintenance. Even strong menswear looks weak when it is wrinkled, dry, misshapen or paired with neglected shoes.

Buying Checklist

  • Accent is muted
  • Only one main accent
  • Base outfit is neutral
  • Color suits season
  • No forced matching

Bottom Line

Using Accent Colors Without Looking Loud should help you make a more confident purchase or styling decision. Choose the version that fits your real routine, works with pieces you already own and can be maintained properly.
