The Overcoat Buying Guide

How to choose an overcoat that fits over tailoring and works across cold-weather outfits.

In This Guide

  1. Overview
  2. What To Check
  3. Common Mistakes
  4. Buying Checklist

Overview

An overcoat must fit over what you actually wear in winter. Try it with a jacket or heavy sweater, not just a T-shirt.

Navy, charcoal and camel are the most versatile colors. Navy is sharp, charcoal is understated and camel adds warmth.

What To Check

Length changes the mood. Knee-length coats feel classic and protective; shorter coats are easier for commuting but less formal.

Check the shoulder and sleeve with layers. A coat that restricts movement will be left at home no matter how good it looks.

Common Mistakes

The easiest mistake is buying the idea of an item instead of judging how it works on your body, in your climate and with your existing wardrobe.

Another mistake is ignoring maintenance. Even strong menswear looks weak when it is wrinkled, dry, misshapen or paired with neglected shoes.

Buying Checklist

  • Fits over jacket
  • Sleeves cover layers
  • Color suits wardrobe
  • Length matches use
  • Fabric feels substantial

Bottom Line

The Overcoat Buying Guide should help you make a more confident purchase or styling decision. Choose the version that fits your real routine, works with pieces you already own and can be maintained properly.
