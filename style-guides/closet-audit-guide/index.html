How To Audit Your Closet Twice A Year

A seasonal system for editing, repairing and replacing clothing before problems become urgent.

In This Guide

  1. Overview
  2. What To Check
  3. Common Mistakes
  4. Buying Checklist

Overview

A closet audit should be practical, not emotional. Look at fit, condition, frequency of wear and outfit compatibility.

Create four groups: keep, tailor, repair and remove. This turns vague dissatisfaction into clear actions.

What To Check

Try questionable pieces with real outfits. If an item never works with anything else, it may not deserve space.

Audit before the season changes so you have time to repair shoes, alter trousers or replace worn shirts.

Common Mistakes

The easiest mistake is buying the idea of an item instead of judging how it works on your body, in your climate and with your existing wardrobe.

Another mistake is ignoring maintenance. Even strong menswear looks weak when it is wrinkled, dry, misshapen or paired with neglected shoes.

Buying Checklist

  • Fit still works
  • Condition is acceptable
  • Item supports outfits
  • Repair needs identified
  • Unused pieces removed

Bottom Line

How To Audit Your Closet Twice A Year should help you make a more confident purchase or styling decision. Choose the version that fits your real routine, works with pieces you already own and can be maintained properly.
