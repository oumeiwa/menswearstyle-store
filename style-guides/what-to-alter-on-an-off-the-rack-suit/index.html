What To Alter On An Off-The-Rack Suit

A clear guide to the alterations worth paying for and the fit problems that are usually not worth fixing.

In This Guide

  1. Overview
  2. What To Check
  3. Common Mistakes
  4. Buying Checklist

Overview

The best off-the-rack suit alterations are small, precise changes. Sleeve length, trouser hem, waist suppression and trouser waist adjustments usually produce the biggest improvement.

Avoid buying a suit with broken shoulders, severe collar gap or a jacket that is much too short. These issues are either expensive or impossible to fix well.

What To Check

Bring your dress shoes and preferred shirt to the tailor. Trouser break and sleeve length depend on the complete outfit, not isolated measurements.

A good tailor should pin both sides, check movement and explain limits. If the suggested work costs more than the suit is worth, keep shopping.

Common Mistakes

The easiest mistake is buying the idea of an item instead of judging how it works on your body, in your climate and with your existing wardrobe.

Another mistake is ignoring maintenance. Even strong menswear looks weak when it is wrinkled, dry, misshapen or paired with neglected shoes.

Buying Checklist

  • Sleeve length can be adjusted cleanly
  • Trouser hem suits your shoes
  • Jacket waist needs only minor shaping
  • Shoulders are already correct
  • Tailor explains what cannot be fixed

Bottom Line

What To Alter On An Off-The-Rack Suit should help you make a more confident purchase or styling decision. Choose the version that fits your real routine, works with pieces you already own and can be maintained properly.
